## What Makes a Good Home Golf Simulator?

Before diving into picks, here's what actually matters when you're spending this kind of money:

**Launch monitor accuracy** is the heart of any simulator. Look for units that measure clubhead speed, ball speed, launch angle, spin rate, and carry distance. Radar-based units (like the Mevo+) and camera-based units (like the SkyTrak+) each have tradeoffs in space requirements and accuracy.

**Software** determines how enjoyable the experience is long-term. The best simulators connect to platforms like E6 Connect, GSPro, or The Golf Club 2019 — each offering dozens of world-famous courses.

**Space requirements** are often overlooked. You need at minimum 10 feet of ceiling height, 15 feet of depth, and 12 feet of width for a comfortable swing. Anything smaller and you'll feel cramped.

**Setup time** matters more than most people think. A simulator you can set up in 10 minutes will get used. One that requires 30 minutes of configuration will collect dust.

## What You Need Beyond the Launch Monitor

Most first-time buyers forget to budget for the rest of the setup. Here's a realistic breakdown:

**Hitting mat ($150–$400):** Don't skip this. A quality mat like the TrueStrike or Fiberbuilt protects your joints and simulates fairway or tee conditions realistically. Cheap foam mats will wreck your wrists.

**Impact screen or net ($200–$800):** Carl's Place is the go-to brand for DIY screens. Their 4K-compatible enclosures start at $299 and are durable enough to take full-speed driver shots. If you don't want to project, a basic net from Rukket ($80–$150) works fine for data-only practice.

**Projector ($400–$800):** You need a short-throw projector to avoid shadows. The BenQ TH671ST is a popular sub-$700 option that delivers a bright, clear image even in a lit room.

**Software subscription ($0–$200/year):** GSPro ($199/year) is the gold standard for course variety and graphics. E6 Connect has a free tier with limited courses and paid tiers up to $299/year. The Golf Club 2019 is a one-time purchase (~$40) with excellent user-created courses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a special room for a golf simulator?**
Any space with at least 9 feet of ceiling height, 10 feet of depth, and 10 feet of width can work. A garage bay is ideal. Basements work well too, as long as there's enough overhead clearance for a full swing.

**Can I use a simulator to actually improve my game?**
Absolutely. The shot data from any of these launch monitors — spin rate, attack angle, face angle — is the same data professional coaches use. Many tour players train with SkyTrak or Mevo+ units in their off-season.

**Are these launch monitors accurate enough to trust?**
The SkyTrak+ and Mevo+ are within 1–3% of premium commercial units like the Trackman 4. For the price difference, that accuracy is more than sufficient for practice and casual simulation. The Garmin R10 and Rapsodo are slightly less precise but still highly useful.

**Will this damage my floors or walls?**
Use a quality mat to protect your floors from divots and club scraping. A proper impact screen or net is essential — never hit full shots at a bare wall or thin net not rated for full-speed shots.
