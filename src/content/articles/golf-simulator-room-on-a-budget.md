## What Does a Golf Simulator Actually Require?

A functional golf simulator needs five things:

1. **A launch monitor** ГўВЂВ” measures your ball speed, launch angle, and spin to calculate shot data
2. **A hitting mat** ГўВЂВ” something to swing off that doesn't destroy your back or your clubs
3. **An impact screen or net** ГўВЂВ” catches your shots (and lets you project onto it if using a projector)
4. **A projector** (optional but highly recommended) ГўВЂВ” displays the sim course on the screen
5. **Simulator software** ГўВЂВ” the virtual course you're playing

You can get a basic functional setup for ~$1,500, a solid mid-tier setup for ~$2,500, and a genuinely excellent experience for ~$3,000. Let's break it down.

## The Hitting Mat: Don't Go Too Cheap

The hitting mat is where you'll spend every session standing and striking. A mat that's too thin will hurt your wrists, a mat that's too slippery will shift between shots, and a mat with bad turf will give you false strike feedback.

## The Projector

If you're going all-in on the simulator experience, the projector matters. You need something bright enough to see in a well-lit room and with a throw distance that works for your space.

### What to Look For

- **Lumens**: 3,000+ lumens minimum for daytime use. 4,000ГўВЂВ“5,000 lumens is ideal
- **Throw ratio**: Short-throw projectors work better in tight spaces. Look for 1.0:1 or lower throw ratio if your projector-to-screen distance is under 8 feet
- **Resolution**: 1080p is plenty for golf simulation. 4K is not necessary
- **Budget**: $300ГўВЂВ“$600 covers a solid 1080p projector for simulator use

**Popular picks in the sim community:**
- **BenQ TH671ST** (~$599) ГўВЂВ” short throw, 3,000 lumens, 1080p ГўВЂВ” a very popular sim projector

## Space Requirements

The minimum realistic ceiling height for a sim setup is **9 feet**. Most golfers are comfortable at 9.5ГўВЂВ“10 feet. If your space has lower ceilings, you may need to use a more compact swing or choose a room with more clearance.

**Horizontal space**: You need at least **15 feet of total depth** ГўВЂВ” roughly 6 feet from the screen to the hitting position, plus 2ГўВЂВ“3 feet of room behind you to swing freely.

**Width**: A 10-foot screen requires roughly 12 feet of room width.

A typical single-car garage works well. A dedicated basement room works even better.

## The Order to Buy

If budget is a concern, buy in this order:

1. **Launch monitor first** ГўВЂВ” this is the investment that can move with you to any setup
2. **Hitting mat second** ГўВЂВ” you can hit into a basic net while you save for a screen
3. **Net or screen + frame third**
4. **Projector last** ГўВЂВ” everything before this is useful without a projector

This approach lets you start practicing with data immediately and build toward the full sim experience over time.
